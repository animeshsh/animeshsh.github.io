Research Timeline

A journey across aerospace, plasma physics, diagnostics, propulsion, simulation, and systems thinking. Each phase builds on the last and expands the scope of the next.

2011

Early Systems Thinking

Built early intuition for logic, code, and systems through programming projects and game design. This was the start of thinking in terms of dynamics, rules, control, and interaction.

2015

BTech in Aeronautical Engineering

Formal grounding in aerospace engineering with exposure to fluid mechanics, propulsion, structures, flight mechanics, and the physical thinking that underpins engineered systems.

2016–2017

Purdue University | MS

Advanced training in aeronautics and astronautics. Built strong foundations in analysis, modeling, and interdisciplinary engineering research.

2016–2020

Laser-Plasma Diagnostics and Experimental Research

Developed microwave scattering diagnostics for laser-induced plasmas and worked with ultrafast laser systems. Combined experiments, signal interpretation, and plasma physics into a coherent research framework.

2017–2020

Purdue University | PhD

Completed a PhD in Aeronautics and Astronautics with deep engagement in plasma science, diagnostics, laser-matter interaction, and research problem solving across theory and experiment.

2020–2022

Postdoc at UIUC | Propulsion and Thruster Systems

Worked on DARPA-funded monopropellant microthruster development. Integrated propulsion, plasma-adjacent physics, testing, design, and hardware realization into a highly applied research program.

2023–2025

IIT Delhi | Plasma Simulation and Large-Scale Computation

Focused on PIC simulations, wave-plasma interactions, stochastic fields, and computational plasma physics using tools such as OSIRIS and EPOCH. Managed large datasets and simulation pipelines while extending analytical and numerical capability.

Along the way

Teaching, Mentoring, and Technical Communication

Taught numerical methods, guided students, built documentation, and translated complex engineering ideas into usable material. This added a strong communication layer to a deeply technical career.

Now

A Unified Engineering Vision

Bringing together drones, aerospace, propulsion, plasma science, simulation, diagnostics, and interactive systems into a single engineering narrative focused on building, explaining, and scaling meaningful technology.
